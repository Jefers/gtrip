Rome Leisurely Sightseeing Adventure
Hey traveler! Get ready for an unforgettable 5-day escape to the Eternal City in early May, where spring blooms make every street feel magical. With your hotel near the iconic Spanish Steps, you're perfectly positioned for easy, relaxed exploration—no rush, just pure Roman vibes. Expect mild weather (highs around 22-25°C/72-77°F, cool evenings), so light layers and comfy shoes are your best friends. Pro tip: Start mornings slow with a cappuccino al banco, and always toss a coin in the Trevi Fountain to ensure your return!

Day 1: Arrival & Heart of Rome – Iconic Fountains and Piazzas
Ease into Rome with nearby gems and charming strolls. Perfect for shaking off jet lag at a leisurely pace.

### Spanish Steps & Piazza di Spagna
Morning: Wake up and step right out to the stunning Spanish Steps (just minutes from your hotel). Climb or simply people-watch from the base—fewer crowds early! Admire the boat-shaped fountain and blooming azaleas in May.

Afternoon: Stroll Via dei Condotti for window shopping (luxury vibes without buying). Grab a gelato nearby and relax in the piazza.

Evening: Wander to the Trevi Fountain (10-15 min walk). Watch the sunset glow on the baroque masterpiece—magical!

Night: Dinner in the area—try classic Roman pasta like cacio e pepe at a cozy trattoria. End with a passeggiata (leisurely evening walk) back to your hotel.

Food Recommendations
* Breakfast: Cornetto and cappuccino at a local bar (~€3-5)
* Lunch: Light panini or supplì from a street spot
* Dinner: Trattoria near Spanish Steps – expect €20-35 per person

Day 2: Ancient Rome – Colosseum, Forum & Palatine Hill
Dive into history without overdoing it—book skip-the-line tickets for a relaxed visit.

### Colosseum
Morning: Head to the Colosseum (metro or 20-30 min walk). Arrive early to beat crowds. Marvel at the massive arena—imagine gladiators!

Afternoon: Explore the Roman Forum and Palatine Hill (included ticket). Wander ancient ruins, temples, and views—take breaks on benches.

Evening: Walk back via Via dei Fori Imperiali for illuminated views.

Night: Relaxed dinner in Monti neighborhood—trendy yet chill vibes.

Tips
* Wear comfy shoes—lots of uneven stones!
* Bring water; May can feel warm in the sun.

Day 3: Vatican City – Art, Basilica & Riverside Views
A full but relaxed day across the Tiber—early start helps avoid lines.

### Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel
Morning: Metro to Ottaviano. Enter Vatican Museums early. Take your time with masterpieces—don't rush the Sistine Chapel!

Afternoon: St. Peter's Basilica (free entry, possible security lines). Climb the dome if energy allows for epic views (extra fee).

Evening: Stroll along the Tiber River or visit Castel Sant'Angelo for sunset panoramas.

Day 4: Centro Storico – Pantheon, Navona & Charming Streets

### Pantheon
Morning: Walk to the Pantheon (15-20 min). Enter the ancient temple (small entry fee)—wow at the oculus and Raphael's tomb!

Afternoon: Piazza Navona with Bernini's Fountain of the Four Rivers. Artists, street performers, gelato breaks.

Day 5: Trastevere & Villa Borghese – Bohemian Charm & Green Escape

### Trastevere
Morning: Cross the river to Trastevere. Wander cobblestone streets, ivy-covered buildings—pure magic in May sunshine.

Afternoon: Head to Villa Borghese gardens (short bus/taxi). Rent bikes or stroll—picnic, people-watch, enjoy views from Pincio Terrace.

Cost Breakdown
* Food & Drinks: €40-80 per day
* Attractions: €60-100
* Total estimated per person (excluding hotel): €150-300 for 5 days

Buon viaggio—Rome awaits!
